The firm, in three statements
i.

We invest by rule, not view.

Models, computation, and data do the work. We hold no house view on the market. No quarterly debates about whether to be bullish. Strategies are designed, tested, deployed, and watched — not voted on.

ii.

We grow through good markets and bad.

Quality, momentum, low-volatility, carry, mean-reversion — each behaves differently in different conditions. Holding them together smooths the ride without giving up the long-run growth. We spread risk across many different bets.

iii.

We work with people who think long-term.

No pitch decks. No quarterly fund-of-the-month. We work with a small number of families and individuals whose horizon matches ours — multi-year, across generations, and patient through the quiet years that growth requires.

Who we work with

Capital is welcome. Mindset is the qualifier.

We work with a deliberately small number of clients. What makes someone a good fit isn’t the size of the cheque — it’s whether we think about markets, time, and evidence the same way. If the following sounds like how you already think, we should talk.

i.

Patient over the multi-year.

Comfortable measuring progress in years, not quarters. Aware that the most useful growth happens in the quiet stretches that never make for something to brag about.

ii.

Interested in how it actually works.

Reads past the headline number. Wants to understand the method, the assumptions, and the limits. Prefers a risk explained to a risk reassured away.

iii.

Resistant to marketing.

Sceptical of star-manager profiles, fund-house tier lists, and flashy quarterly numbers. Trusts method, not personalities; evidence, not narrative.

iv.

Comfortable with drawdowns.

Knows that any strategy with real long-run returns will have uncomfortable stretches. Has the capacity — financial and temperamental — to hold through them.

v.

Wants a partner, not a product.

Wants an ongoing relationship, not a quarterly statement. Comfortable sharing the rest of the financial picture — insurance, real estate, business interests — because none of it is separate from the portfolio.

vi.

Global in outlook.

Thinks beyond Indian equities as the only thing worth owning. Open to a multi-asset, multi-geography, multi-strategy approach — without giving up on the Indian growth story.
