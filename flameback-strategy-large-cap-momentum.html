viMethodology

How it works.

A rules-based momentum ranking, applied to a clean universe, rebalanced on a fixed schedule. No discretion at the trade level.

At every monthly rebalance, the strategy ranks the NSE 100 universe by a composite momentum signal — multi-period price momentum, adjusted for volatility, with a recent-month skip to dampen short-term mean reversion noise.

The top-ranked names are selected for the portfolio, typically 15 to 20 positions. Position weights are set by inverse volatility — smaller weights on more volatile names, larger weights on steadier ones — with a single-name cap to limit concentration. Turnover constraints prevent excessive churn between rebalances.

Between rebalances, the strategy holds its positions as they are. Names that fall through pre-defined trigger levels exit through trailing stops, and they are not bought back until they re-qualify at the next rebalance. The intent is plain: ride trends while they last, and exit cleanly when they end.

The strategy does not use leverage, short positions, or derivatives. Those approaches sit inside the QX Funds wrapper. For PMS clients who want explicit downside protection, the firm can pair the Large-cap Momentum allocation with an index-option overlay separately as part of a wider mandate.

viiiRisks

What can go wrong.

A long-only equity strategy carries clear risks. We manage them at the strategy level; we do not pretend to avoid them. Read them carefully before, during, and after you invest.

i

Market risk

Equities are volatile. Diversified large-caps have lost 20–30% within months in stress periods. The strategy participates fully in those declines — there are no hedges, no cash buffer, and no derivatives. The drawdown table is the documented record.

ii

Momentum reversal

Momentum strategies can experience abrupt reversals at sharp turning points — yesterday’s winners become today’s losers. Trailing stops and the next rebalance correct for this, but exit signals can lag a fast reversal by weeks. The cost shows up in transition months.

iii

Mean-reverting markets

When markets favour laggards over leaders, the strategy underperforms. 2022 is the most recent example: the strategy returned +0.8% while the benchmark returned +4.3%. Such periods can extend twelve to eighteen months and test client patience meaningfully.

iv

Concentration

The portfolio holds 15–20 names, with sector tilts driven by where momentum currently is — presently 28% in Financial Services. Single-name shocks (corporate action, accounting issue, regulatory event) or sector shocks (policy change, commodity move) can move the portfolio materially.

v

Behavioural risk

The strategy compounds only because investors stay invested through drawdowns. Selling at the bottom locks in losses. Clients without a five-year-plus horizon may struggle to hold through periods like March 2020 (−24.5%) or the 2022 sideways year. Mandate fit matters more than strategy fit.

vi

Factor crowding

As momentum strategies have become more widely adopted globally, the factor itself can experience crowded-trade dynamics — sharp drawdowns when many participants exit similar positions simultaneously. This risk is most acute around macro inflection points and policy shifts.

vii

Tax and turnover

Annualised turnover near 80% means more taxable events than a buy-and-hold portfolio. Short-term capital gains tax can be a meaningful drag on net-of-tax returns for clients in higher tax brackets. All performance shown on this page is pre-tax.

viii

Operational and structural

The PMS structure relies on third-party custody, broker execution, and systems uptime. SEBI regulations on Portfolio Managers may change. None of these are unique to the strategy, but they are real and outside the strategy team’s direct control.

The strategy is not designed to avoid losses — it is designed to compound through cycles by participating in equity markets with discipline. Clients who cannot accept the risks above should consider lower-volatility offerings, or speak with the firm about a blended mandate that includes hedges or fixed-income allocation.
