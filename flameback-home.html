iOur process

How we build a strategy.

We look for patterns in the market, turn them into tested rules, and run them with discipline. The same four steps, on every strategy.

i.

Research

We look for patterns that have actually driven returns — in prices, company fundamentals, and the wider economy.

ii.

Write the rules

We turn each pattern into exact rules a computer can follow, with no room for guesswork.

iii.

Test it

We test on data the rules have never seen. We keep what holds up and drop what doesn’t.

iv.

Run it live

We put the strategy to work with real money, and watch it closely.

iiThe firm, in three statements

What this actually means.

Three plain statements about how we work.

i.

We invest like engineers.

We use models, data, and computing power. We don’t take a house view on the market or argue about whether to be bullish. Strategies are designed and tested — not voted on.

ii.

We aim to compound through every market.

Different approaches work in different conditions. We combine several of them, so the ride is smoother — without giving up long-term growth.

iii.

We work with families who think long-term.

No pitch decks, no fund-of-the-month. We work with a small number of families whose time horizon matches ours — measured in years, even generations.

viWho we work with

We work with a small number of families. What makes someone a good fit isn’t the size of the cheque — it’s how they think about markets, time, and evidence. If this sounds like you, we should talk.

i.

Patient over years.

You measure progress in years, not quarters. You know the best compounding often happens in the quiet stretches.

ii.

Curious how it works.

You read past the headline number. You want to understand the method, the assumptions, and the risks — not just be reassured.

iii.

Wary of marketing.

You’re sceptical of star managers and quarterly performance theatre. You trust method and evidence over personalities and stories.

iv.

You know any strategy worth holding will have hard stretches — and you can hold through them, financially and temperamentally.

v.

viiFAQ

Common questions.

The questions people actually ask, answered plainly.

Where are client investments held?
In an account in your own name, with SEBI-registered brokers or custodians. We never hold your money ourselves.
How long does onboarding take?
A first conversation can usually happen within the week. KYC and account setup take two to three business days once we’ve agreed to work together.
Is there a lock-in?
No lock-in. We suggest giving it at least three years to work — but you can leave at any time.
How are portfolios reported on?
A live dashboard with daily NAV and holdings, plus formal statements as required by the relevant regulator.
Does the firm publish performance?
Yes — to registered users. The numbers aren’t on the open web, but anyone who creates an account can see the full record.
What does the firm not do?
No commission products. No insurance sales. No real estate. No startup deals. Managing wealth is all we do.
Who owns the firm?
The founders, employees, clients, and well-wishers own the firm. No VC, no PE, no bank. Because clients can own a piece of their own wealth manager, we can make long-term decisions without pressure from outside money.

The first conversation is just a conversation.

No slides, no pitch, no commitment. We spend most of it asking questions — and we’ll tell you honestly if we’re not a fit. Plenty of first conversations end right there. That’s fine.
