i. THE FRAME
Why this page is called
‘Our work’

This is not a product catalogue. It is what the firm makes.

a.Work, not products

Most wealth-management firms call this page Products. The word is honest, in its way — it names the underlying activity, which is to package investment strategies into containers that can be sold and priced. We use a different word because we do a different thing. The strategies on this page are the firm’s craft.

Each one is a piece of working code that came out of the research funnel. Each one was selected, sized, and deployed by the people on the team page. Each one carries founders’ capital alongside client capital. None of them is marketed in the usual sense — no commissions for advisors to push them, no magazine ads, no quarterly product launches.

The list below is what the firm is for. It is, more than anything, our answer to the question every serious prospective client should ask: what, exactly, are you going to do with my capital?

The firm’s eighteen strategies share one architecture — the same stages of universe, signal, risk, construction, and execution. They differ in which signals they use and how they weight them. The strategy below, Momentum of Velocity, is shown in full so the architecture is clear. The others are listed in the catalogue that follows.

Featured strategy

Momentum of Velocity.

An Indian-equity strategy that picks stocks on accelerating momentum — the rate of change of momentum, not the level — filtered through a quality gate and sized by realized-volatility risk-budgeting. Sector caps, drawdown control, and capacity-aware execution come after.

The idea: a stock that is moving and also speeding up behaves differently from one moving at a steady rate. The first usually keeps going; the second usually reverts. The premium is the difference.

UNIVERSEIndian listed equity, NSE + BSE
STYLELong-only, factor-driven, weekly rebalanced
HORIZON3–5 years, intended for patient capital
LIVE SINCEOctober 2018, founders’ book first
CAPACITYDefined; published in the factsheet
FACTSHEETOpen the factsheet →
ArchitectureThe strategy as eight sequential layers, top to bottom.
i.
INPUT LAYERUniverse.
The full set of listed Indian equities meeting basic liquidity and listing-age requirements. Adjusted weekly. The starting pool from which everything downstream selects.
In: ~3,000 listed equities
Out: ~1,200 liquidity-cleared
ii.
FILTERQuality gate.
A fundamentals screen: ROE consistency, debt-to-equity, earnings stability, accrual quality. Removes companies the strategy should not hold, whatever the price is doing.
In: ~1,200
Out: ~500 quality-cleared
iii.
PRIMARY SIGNALMomentum.
The standard 12-month-minus-1-month total return. Every name in the cleared universe is scored. This is the textbook factor; the strategy’s edge is what comes next.
In: ~500 quality names
Out: ranked by momentum
iv.
PROPRIETARY OVERLAYVelocity overlay.
The rate of change of momentum itself. Names whose momentum is accelerating score higher than names at the same momentum level whose momentum is fading. This is what makes the strategy distinct.
In: momentum-ranked
Out: velocity-adjusted ranks
v.
RISK WEIGHTINGInverse-vol sizing.
Position sizes are set inversely to realized 90-day volatility. Higher-vol names get smaller positions for the same conviction. Each name contributes roughly equal risk.
In: ranked candidates
Out: risk-budgeted weights
vi.
PORTFOLIO LIMITSConstruction gate.
Single-name cap, sector cap, correlation overlay. Keeps the strategy from concentrating in correlated bets even when the signal says yes. It is not allowed to turn into a sector trade.
In: risk-budgeted
Out: constraint-feasible portfolio
vii.
DRAWDOWN CONTROLDe-gross rule.
If realized drawdown passes a set threshold, the strategy cuts gross exposure automatically. It re-grosses by rule, not by mood. The client is not asked. The system does it.
In: intended portfolio
Out: net-of-DD-rule positions
viii.
OUTPUT LAYERExecution.
Capacity-aware orders routed through FIST to the firm’s brokers. VWAP and implementation-shortfall algos depending on size and liquidity. Every order is logged. Every fill is reconciled.
In: target positions
Out: live client capital
The big money is not in the buying and selling. It is in the waiting. — Charles T. Munger, with the firm’s preferred emphasis on the last clause.

iv. READING
How to look at
any factsheet on this site

Six numbers on every factsheet. None of them is hidden.

a.The reporting discipline

Every strategy factsheet on this site carries the same six numbers, calculated the same way, reported over the same windows (1-year, 3-year, 5-year, since-inception). The firm has no favourite window. The strategies are what they are. The factsheets show what they show.

i.

Compounded annual return.

Net of fees, taxes, and transaction costs. Reported side by side across periods, so one strong period doesn’t hide the others.

ii.

Realized volatility.

Annualized standard deviation of monthly returns. Not the same as risk, but a useful summary of how much the strategy moves.

iii.

Sharpe ratio.

Return per unit of volatility, net of the risk-free rate. Useful for comparison; not the whole picture.

iv.

Maximum drawdown.

The deepest peak-to-trough decline the strategy has experienced live. Reported alongside the date range it occurred in.

v.

Time to recovery.

How long it took, after the deepest drawdown, to reach a new high. Often the most useful number for a patient client.

vi.

Worst calendar year.

Plain January-to-December performance in the strategy’s worst observed year. Not the worst rolling 12 months — that’s a separate number.

v. LIFECYCLE
How a strategy enters,
lives in, leaves a portfolio

The journey a strategy makes into your portfolio.

a.The journey, in five steps

A strategy doesn’t enter your portfolio just because it appears on this page. Each one is allocated, sized, monitored, and eventually retired according to the lifecycle below. The discipline is on the firm. Your only job is patience.

i.

The strategy survives R&D.

It passes hypothesis, back-test, walk-forward, regime testing, and paper trading, then runs on the firm’s own capital for six to twelve months. See the Strategy R&D page for the funnel in detail.

ii.

It is allocated to a client mandate.

Strategies are allocated to your portfolio based on your liabilities, horizon, behaviour, and stated mandate — not as a menu to choose from. A young saver and a retired estate get different mixes of the same underlying eighteen.

iii.

It is sized by the strategy’s live track record.

New live strategies start at small allocations and earn their weight over time. A strategy with eighteen months of live capital does not get the same allocation as one with eight years.

iv.

It is monitored against its pre-registered behaviour.

Every live strategy has a written expectation: target Sharpe, expected drawdown profile, expected behaviour by regime. Quarterly reviews compare live results to that expectation; large gaps trigger investigation.

v.

It is retired when the evidence says so.

Signals decay. Capacity changes. Market structure shifts. When a strategy drifts far from its pre-registered behaviour — over a long enough period to be meaningful, not after one bad quarter — the firm retires it. That capital moves to the strategies still earning their place.
