What we do i. Private wealth management ii. Advisory iii. QX Funds

One way of thinking, in three different forms.

The firm exists to do one thing: help patient capital compound through engineered systems. The three sections below are the shapes that work takes, depending on what you actually need.

what.
i.Private wealth management

The heart of the firm.

A long-term partnership in which the firm manages your capital across global markets, using its own quantitative strategies, in a portfolio designed specifically for you. Below: how it actually works, in four steps.

i.

We start by listening.

Before any number is exchanged, before any product is mentioned, before any strategy is named — we spend time understanding you. What you’ve built, what you’re building, where the capital came from, what you want it to do. Not as a sales call, but as the actual work.

Most wealth firms do this the other way around — they lead with a product and fit you to it. We find the result is worse for everyone. A portfolio that doesn’t know who it’s for tends to be sold rather than built.

“The thirty minutes we spent on what my parents went through — that’s when I knew this firm was different.” — a client, after a first conversation

ii.

We look at the whole picture.

An investment portfolio doesn’t sit on its own. It sits inside a life that includes real estate, businesses, family commitments, insurance cover, tax, ESOPs or RSUs in play, lending facilities, sometimes property abroad, sometimes inheritance still in motion. None of these can be separated from the portfolio.

The firm engages with all of them — not by selling you more products, but by understanding the picture well enough that the portfolio we build is the right one for the situation you are actually in. If a fifth of your net worth is tied up in unvested ESOPs, the equity part of the portfolio should not behave as if it isn’t.

Things we look at, alongside the portfolio
  • Real estate holdings & rental income
  • Business interests & concentration risk
  • Insurance cover & liability exposure
  • ESOPs, RSUs, founder equity
  • Existing debt & lending lines
  • Family commitments & lifecycle events
  • Tax position & carry-forward losses
  • Estate planning & succession
Each of these informs the portfolio design. None of them are separately invoiced.
iii.

We design for you, not for a model portfolio.

The firm runs eighteen live strategies. The portfolio we build for you is a custom blend drawn from those strategies, weighted to your specific picture — horizon, liquidity needs, existing exposures, geographic balance, tax position, drawdown tolerance.

Two clients with similar net worth often end up with very different portfolios, because their lives are different. The strategies are standard; the allocations are not.

The mandate is global. Indian equities, US equities, multi-asset where it fits. The exact balance follows where your wealth and your goals sit — for clients with most of their wealth in India, the default leans toward Indian exposure, but the global piece is always there, sized to you.

iv.

We manage the account, not just the trades.

Once the portfolio is live, the work is ongoing. Strategies rebalance on their own cadence. The firm watches the whole picture continuously. A business sale, a property transaction, a change in tax residency, a planned liquidity event — each is a reason to revisit the design.

How often we talk is up to you. Some clients want a quarterly review and otherwise prefer to be left alone. Others speak with us monthly. The default isn’t a calendar; it’s whatever matches how involved you want to be.

Underneath the conversation, the systems keep working. We do not call you to talk about market moves. If a strategy is doing what it was designed to do, however uncomfortable that feels, the right thing is to let it.

ii.Advisory

The work that surrounds wealth management.

Many clients first come to us for a single piece of work — an ESOP plan, a business exit, a property transaction — and only later move into ongoing wealth management. Others use the firm only for these one-off projects. Either is welcome.

Advisory is how the firm handles capital and financial decisions outside the day-to-day management of an investment portfolio. The same engineering-first approach applies — careful analysis, clear assumptions, recommendations we can defend — but the work is project-based rather than ongoing.

For clients who eventually move into wealth management, advisory work is often the first real interaction with the firm. For those who don’t, it stands on its own.

i.

Financial planning.

Retirement modelling, education funding, planning for major life events, cash-flow projections. Numbers where they matter, judgment where they don’t.

ii.

Business restructuring & exits.

Helping founders and promoters structure exits, secondary sales, and ownership transitions. Hedging the concentrated-position risk that precedes most material exits.

iii.

ESOP & RSU planning.

Tax-aware exercise scheduling, currency exposure for global plans, hedging strategies against unvested equity risk, and integration with the rest of the household balance sheet.

iv.

Real estate & lending.

Property purchase analysis, financing-vs-cash trade-offs, structuring of LAS facilities and loans against shares. Closing assistance on material transactions.

v.

Asset administration.

De-mat conversions, share consolidation across folios, dormant-account reactivation, physical-to-electronic transitions. The unglamorous but necessary housekeeping of a complex balance sheet.

vi.

Estate & succession.

Wills, trusts, intergenerational wealth structuring, family-office set-up considerations. Working with the family’s lawyers and accountants rather than replacing them.

iii.QX Funds

A pooled expression of the same engine.

For investors who want the firm’s strategies in a pooled vehicle rather than a managed account — usually because they don’t want a custom mandate, or because the pooled structure is more efficient for them.

QX Equanimity is the firm’s flagship pooled vehicle — a Category III AIF running a systematic, quality-momentum blend across Indian large-caps. The same engine, the same R&D process, the same eight-year history. What’s different is the structure.

In private wealth management, the firm designs a portfolio for you. In QX Funds, you subscribe to a portfolio that already exists. The strategy is fixed. The allocation is the same for every investor. The flexibility is in the structure, not in the construction.

QX exists for two reasons. First, some investors prefer the pooled structure — the regulatory framework, the audit treatment, the standard terms. Second, some allocations are too small or too complex to administer as a separate mandate, and the pooled vehicle is the right home for them.

The firm runs other QX vehicles for specific strategies, but Equanimity is the flagship.
