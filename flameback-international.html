iThe routes

Two paths. One managed portfolio.

Indian residents access global markets through the Liberalised Remittance Scheme. NRIs and OCI cardholders access them through global accounts directly, or through a GIFT City vehicle.

Two routes — LRS for residents, direct or GIFT City for NRIs — converging to a managed dollar portfolio

For Indian residents, the route runs through the Liberalised Remittance Scheme. Each individual can remit up to USD 250,000 per financial year for permitted transactions including overseas investments. Funds move from the client’s Indian bank account to their own IBKR USD account, which holds securities in the client’s name throughout. Flameback acts as Investment Adviser; the broker holds custody.

For NRIs and OCI cardholders, the route is more direct. Clients with existing US or global accounts can have Flameback advise on those directly. For clients wanting an Indian-domiciled wrapper, the GIFT City IFSC vehicle is the alternative — a fund structure regulated by IFSCA that holds global assets for NRI and accredited investors.

iiiHow it works

From onboarding to deployment.

A few weeks end to end, with the bulk of the time on account opening and remittance.

Conversation & profileInitial conversation to understand currency goals, time horizon, existing global exposure, and tax position. Profile and portfolio shortlisted.
KYC & account openingKYC with Flameback and account opening with IBKR (or the chosen platform). For NRIs, existing accounts can usually be used.
Remittance & fundingFor LRS clients, INR is remitted to USD into the IBKR account through the client’s bank. The bank handles A2 Form and FEMA documentation.
Portfolio construction & deploymentPortfolio constructed and deployed into the account. Phased deployment is the default for lump sums above a threshold, to manage entry timing.
Advisory & reviewTrades are recommended by Flameback and approved by the client. Quarterly reviews keep the mandate current. Monthly reporting through the IBKR statements plus Flameback’s performance overlay.
Tax filing & complianceYear-end tax documents from IBKR — required for both Indian and US tax filings. The team assists with the documentation, though tax filing remains the client’s responsibility.

Because the relationship is advisory rather than discretionary, every trade in the client’s international account passes through the client for approval — the same gate as the Advisory service, applied to a USD portfolio.

vMinimum & eligibility

From $25,000.

A practical minimum to make cross-border investing worthwhile after costs. Eligibility depends on the route — LRS for residents, NRI status for direct accounts.

The recommended minimum for an International advisory portfolio is USD 25,000, roughly ₹20 lakh at current rates. The figure is practical, not regulatory — below it, the fixed costs of cross-border investing eat into returns. There is no maximum, and many clients run well above the minimum.

For Indian residents, eligibility is simple: any individual with a PAN, an Indian bank account, and the willingness to complete the LRS remittance paperwork can take part. The $250,000 per-year LRS cap is the only structural ceiling on flows.

NRIs and OCI cardholders should already hold, or be willing to open, a global brokerage account. Tax residency, double-taxation treaty position, and FATCA disclosures need attention up front. The team helps map the right path in the first conversation.

Many clients add an International allocation alongside an existing PMS or Advisory portfolio — treating the two as complementary, with the Indian portfolio building rupee wealth and the international portfolio building dollar wealth, often in parallel.
