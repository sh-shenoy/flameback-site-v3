Section 01

Disclaimer.

This Disclosure Document has been prepared in accordance with the SEBI (Portfolio Managers) Regulations, 2020 and has been filed with the Securities and Exchange Board of India. The purpose of the document is to provide essential information about the portfolio management services offered by Flameback Capital Private Limited in a manner that assists investors in making an informed decision about engaging the Portfolio Manager.

This document has neither been approved nor disapproved by SEBI, nor has SEBI certified the accuracy or adequacy of the contents of this document. The Portfolio Manager has, however, taken reasonable care to ensure that the information provided is true and not misleading.

Investments in securities are subject to market risks. The value of the portfolio may rise or fall depending on factors and forces affecting the securities markets. There can be no assurance that the objectives of the Portfolio Manager will be achieved, and the Portfolio Manager makes no guarantee, express or implied, of any specific return on the investments managed.

Section 05

Services offered.

5.1 · Discretionary portfolio management

Under discretionary portfolio management, the Portfolio Manager exercises full discretion in selecting, allocating, and managing investments on behalf of the Client, in accordance with the agreed Strategy and within the constraints stated in the Agreement. The Client’s consent for individual transactions is not required.

5.2 · Non-discretionary portfolio management

Under non-discretionary portfolio management, the Portfolio Manager provides recommendations, research, and execution support; investment decisions remain with the Client. The Portfolio Manager does not execute transactions without specific Client instruction.

5.3 · Advisory portfolio management

Where requested, the Portfolio Manager provides standalone advisory services in respect of the Client’s portfolio, without taking custody or executing transactions. Such advisory services are governed by a separate engagement letter and are subject to the regulations applicable to SEBI-registered Investment Advisers.

5.4 · Strategies offered

The Portfolio Manager offers a range of equity, multi-asset, and overlay strategies, each with a defined investment objective, universe, and risk profile. A complete list of currently available strategies, along with their individual factsheets, is published at flameback.capital/our-work. The list and details of strategies may be updated from time to time; the current Agreement and accompanying annexure will identify the strategies applicable to the Client.

5.5 · Investment objectives and policies

The investment objective for each Strategy is to generate risk-adjusted returns consistent with the stated Strategy profile, net of fees and expenses, over a defined investment horizon. The Portfolio Manager follows a quantitative, rules-based approach to portfolio construction and risk management. The Portfolio Manager does not guarantee any specific return.

5.6 · Minimum investment and lock-in

The minimum investment for portfolio management services is the higher of (a) the regulatory minimum prescribed under the Regulations from time to time, or (b) the Strategy-specific minimum disclosed in the Agreement. Currently, the regulatory minimum is ₹50,00,000 (Indian Rupees Fifty Lakh) per Client. There is no contractual lock-in period unless specifically stated in the Agreement; however, the Portfolio Manager generally invests with a multi-year horizon, and early withdrawal may impair the Client’s investment outcomes.

Section 06

Risk factors.

An investment in a portfolio managed by the Portfolio Manager involves several risks. The Client should consider, among others, the following:

  • Market risk. The value of investments may decline due to factors affecting securities markets generally or specific issuers.
  • Strategy risk. A Strategy may underperform its benchmark or its historical performance; quantitative models may decay over time or behave unexpectedly in unobserved market regimes.
  • Liquidity risk. Some securities may be illiquid, and disposal under stressed market conditions may incur losses.
  • Concentration risk. Strategy-level concentration in particular securities, sectors, or factors may amplify losses in adverse conditions.
  • Operational and technology risk. Failures in systems, data feeds, execution, custody, or counterparties may affect Client outcomes.
  • Regulatory and tax risk. Changes in regulation or tax law may affect the operation, profitability, or after-tax return of a Strategy.
  • Counterparty risk. Failure of a custodian, broker, exchange, or other counterparty may impact the Client’s assets.
  • Currency risk. For Strategies with cross-border exposure, currency movement may amplify or offset gains and losses.
  • Modelling risk. Quantitative models rely on historical data and assumptions; their results may not be predictive of future outcomes.

The above list is not exhaustive. Detailed risk factors specific to each Strategy are disclosed in the Strategy’s factsheet and in the Agreement.

Section 10

Nature of expenses.

10.1 · Management fees

The Portfolio Manager charges management fees in accordance with the Agreement. The fee model is fixed-fee, variable-fee with a high-water-mark performance component, or a combination of the two, as agreed with each Client. The Portfolio Manager does not accept commissions, rebates, or other consideration from product manufacturers in respect of any investment made on behalf of any Client.

10.2 · Custodian, brokerage, and exchange charges

The Client bears, at actuals, the charges levied by the custodian, brokers, exchanges, depositories, and other counterparties involved in the execution and settlement of transactions on the Client’s account. These charges are not retained by the Portfolio Manager.

10.3 · Statutory and other charges

Statutory levies including securities transaction tax (STT), goods and services tax (GST), stamp duty, and other applicable taxes are charged to the Client’s account at actuals.

Section 12

Accounting policies.

The Portfolio Manager follows the accounting policies prescribed under the Regulations and, where not specifically prescribed, generally accepted Indian accounting principles. Key policies include:

  • Recognition of investments. Investments are recognised at cost on the trade date.
  • Valuation. Listed securities are valued at the closing market price on the relevant stock exchange on each valuation date.
  • NAV computation. The Net Asset Value of each Client’s portfolio is computed on a daily basis (excluding non-business days), net of fees and expenses.
  • Income recognition. Dividend income is recognised on the ex-dividend date; interest income is recognised on accrual.
  • Realised gains and losses. Computed on a first-in-first-out basis unless otherwise specified.
  • Corporate actions. Bonus, split, rights, and other corporate actions are accounted for on the ex-date.
