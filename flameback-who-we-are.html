How do you stay invested when the world looks frightening?

It’s the question we come to work to answer. Our answer, so far, hasn’t been to predict the market or pick its winners — it’s been to build strategies that hold up in markets we haven’t seen yet, run by a team that builds its own tools, with our own money compounding right alongside our clients’.

who.
i.The firm

A firm that runs on engineering, not salesmanship.

Flameback manages the wealth of high-net-worth Indians with a global outlook. We started in 2017, we’re based in Bangalore, and we’re built on a simple belief: done well, wealth management looks more like engineering than salesmanship.

We aim for safety and steady compounding in every kind of market. Each client’s portfolio is personal, but the method underneath is the same everywhere — models, computation, and an edge in the data, refined over what is now an eight-year live history.

We don’t forecast whether the market goes up or down. We don’t pick stocks the traditional way. We build strategies meant to behave acceptably in markets we haven’t seen yet — rather than perfectly in the ones we already have.

We’ve never taken venture capital, private equity, or any institutional money. The firm runs on the founders’ own capital and on a few patient private investors — many of them clients who knew us well before they backed us. Our money grows alongside yours.

The deeper reason this matters is simple. Markets, politics, and technology all go through genuinely frightening stretches, and nobody knows how they end. So how does a thoughtful person find the confidence to invest, and the steadiness to stay invested? That’s the question the firm exists to answer.

iii.Investment philosophy

Three things the firm is built around.

There are countless ways to be wrong in markets. We’re built to be wrong less expensively and right more durably — through structure rather than hunches, repetition rather than conviction.

i.

We don’t predict.

We hold no view on whether the Nifty ends the year up, down, or flat for a decade. We don’t need one. The strategies are built to behave acceptably in markets we haven’t seen yet — not perfectly in the ones we have.

No forecasts · No house view
ii.

We spread our sources of return.

Quality, momentum, low-volatility, carry, mean-reversion — each is a different thing, and each behaves differently. Running them together smooths the ride without giving up the long-run return.

Multi-strategy · Risk-budgeted
iii.

We let the systems do their job.

Once a strategy is live, the discipline is to not second-guess it. A drawdown is a reason to hold the line, not to overrule the model. Eight years tell us this is harder than it sounds — and worth it.

iv.Strategy R&D

Research before risk.

Most ideas we test never become strategies. That’s by design — the job is to kill ideas quickly, so the few that survive have earned the right to manage your money.

i.
Idea
~1 week 100% start here
ii.
Backtest
~2 weeks ~30% survive
iii.
Out-of-sample
~1 month ~12% survive
iv.
Paper trade
~3–6 months ~5% survive
v.
Live
indefinite ~2% reach this

Of every hundred ideas we start testing, about two end up running real money. The other ninety-eight fail somewhere along the way, or get shelved as just not good enough to take a risk on.

Each stage has its own bar. A strategy doesn’t pass the backtest because it looked good on past data. It passes because it held up under walk-forward testing, across different market conditions, under realistic trading costs — and because the reason it should work makes economic sense. The same goes at every stage after.

This is the part of the work that compounds. Every shelved idea teaches us something about what doesn’t travel; every live strategy is a bet we can still defend years later.

v.Technology

Eight years of compounding code.

Our technology isn’t a thing that supports the investing — it is the investing. There’s no real line between a strategy and the system that runs it.

FIST · the trade engine

The execution spine.

Live since 2017. It generates orders, allocates across mandates, applies the risk overlay, and reconciles every fill. Every order we place goes through it.

The data lake · where research starts

The research substrate.

Clean, point-in-time-correct data across Indian and US equities, fundamentals, and the economy. Point-in-time matters more than the industry lets on — it’s the difference between a real backtest and a flattering one.

QX · the portfolio layer

The portfolio layer.

Turns strategy signals into actual portfolios under each client’s constraints — mandate, risk limits, tax.
