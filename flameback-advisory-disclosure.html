Section 01

Disclaimer.

This Disclosure Document has been prepared in accordance with the SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations, 2013, as amended, and the circulars issued thereunder. The purpose of the document is to provide essential information about the investment advisory services offered by Flameback Capital Private Limited in a manner that assists prospective and existing clients in making an informed decision about engaging the Investment Adviser.

This document has neither been approved nor disapproved by SEBI; SEBI has not certified the accuracy or adequacy of its contents. The Investment Adviser has, however, taken reasonable care to ensure that the information provided is true, fair, and not misleading.

Investments are subject to market risks. The Investment Adviser does not guarantee any specific return on the investments recommended, and the Client should read all scheme-related documents and product literature carefully before investing.

Section 04

Business activities.

4.1 · Principal activity

The Investment Adviser’s principal activity is the provision of investment advice to clients in respect of investment products, including equity, debt, mutual funds, exchange-traded funds, alternative investment funds, and other securities permitted under the Regulations. The advice is provided on a non-discretionary basis, with implementation remaining with the Client.

4.2 · Other activities of the company

The Investment Adviser’s holding company and group entities are also engaged in (a) portfolio management services under SEBI registration INP000007358; (b) operation of a Category III Alternative Investment Fund under SEBI registration [insert]; and (c) advisory services to non-Indian clients via a separate U.S. SEC-registered entity (file no. 801-129576). The activities of the Investment Adviser business and the Portfolio Manager business are kept separate as required under the Regulations, including by way of separate client onboarding, separate fee structures, and separate record-keeping.

4.3 · Activities not undertaken

The Investment Adviser does not engage in distribution of any third-party financial product, does not earn commissions, brokerage, or referral fees from any product manufacturer, and does not hold custody of Client funds or securities. The firm operates on a fee-only basis.

Section 07

Conflicts of interest and how managed.

7.1 · The structural approach

The Investment Adviser operates a fee-only business and earns no consideration from product manufacturers. There is therefore no direct commercial incentive to recommend one product over another. Nevertheless, certain conflicts can arise structurally, and the firm has adopted the following policies to identify, manage, and disclose them.

7.2 · Potential conflicts and mitigants

Potential conflictHow it is managed
Cross-selling within groupInvestment advice and portfolio management are operated as separate businesses with separate client agreements and separate fee structures. Where a Client engages with both, each engagement is documented, billed, and reported separately. Cross-introduction never occurs without informed Client consent.
Advice on group products (AIF, PMS)Where the Investment Adviser advises a Client on a product offered by a group entity, the relationship is disclosed in writing before the advice is rendered, and the Client’s informed consent is recorded.
Personal account dealings by employeesAll persons associated with investment advice are subject to a personal account dealing policy. Trades are pre-cleared, restricted to a published universe, and monitored quarterly.
Research and advice integrityResearch outputs and advice recommendations are reviewed by senior personnel independent of any sales or onboarding function. No part of an employee’s compensation is contingent on the recommendation of a particular product or strategy.
Receipt of any incentive from third partiesThe Investment Adviser does not accept commissions, rebates, soft-dollar arrangements, or other consideration from product manufacturers. Any incidental receipt is to be disclosed to the Client and offset against the Client’s fees.

7.3 · Disclosure to the Client

Material conflicts of interest specific to a Client’s engagement are disclosed in writing before the relevant advice is rendered. The Client is asked to record informed consent prior to acting on advice in any situation involving a disclosed conflict.

Section 08

Services offered.

8.1 · Nature of advice

The Investment Adviser provides investment advice on a non-discretionary basis. The Client retains complete authority over investment decisions and their implementation; the Investment Adviser does not execute transactions or hold custody of Client assets.

8.2 · Scope

  • Asset allocation advice across equity, debt, gold, and alternative asset classes, tailored to the Client’s liabilities, horizon, and risk profile;
  • Product selection advice within the Client’s chosen asset classes — mutual funds, exchange-traded funds, listed equity, fixed-income, and securities permitted under the Regulations;
  • Portfolio construction advice incorporating tax efficiency, costs, and the Client’s stated constraints;
  • Ongoing review of the advised portfolio, with periodic written reports;
  • Advice on financial planning matters incidental to investment decisions, including cash-flow planning, retirement projection, succession planning, and the structuring of trust arrangements;
  • Education-grade content, research notes, and writing made available to Clients to support their understanding of the recommended positions.

8.3 · Out of scope

The following are explicitly outside the scope of the Investment Adviser’s services:

  • Tax filing or legal opinion services. The Client must retain separate tax and legal advisers;
  • Brokerage, custody, or execution of any transaction;
  • Distribution of any third-party product;
  • Personalised security selection beyond the scope of the listed asset classes;
  • Discretionary portfolio management — provided only by the affiliated Portfolio Manager under a separate Agreement, with separate disclosures.
Section 09

Fees and payment terms.

9.1 · Fee model

The Investment Adviser charges fees in one of two modes prescribed under the Regulations:

  1. Assets-under-advice (AUA) basis: a fee not exceeding 2.5% per annum (or the prevailing ceiling prescribed by SEBI) of the assets under advice, computed and billed in accordance with the Agreement;
  2. Fixed-fee basis: a fixed annual fee not exceeding the ceiling prescribed by SEBI for fixed-fee engagements, irrespective of the assets under advice.

The exact fee, fee basis, computation, and frequency are set out in the engagement letter executed with each Client. The Client may elect to switch between the two modes only after twelve months from the date of fee election or as otherwise permitted by SEBI.

9.2 · What is included and excluded

The advisory fee covers the services described in section 8.2. Statutory levies (including GST), the costs of any third-party services the Client elects to use (such as custodians, brokers, or auditors), and the cost of any product the Client invests in (such as scheme expenses of a mutual fund) are not included in the advisory fee and are borne by the Client at actuals.

9.3 · Mode of payment

Fees are paid by the Client directly to the Investment Adviser by bank transfer, cheque, UPI, or other electronic means agreed to in writing. The Investment Adviser does not accept fees in cash and does not accept fees via any product manufacturer.

Section 10

Risk profiling and suitability.

10.1 · The process

Before any advice is rendered, the Investment Adviser conducts a risk-profiling exercise covering the Client’s financial circumstances, investment objectives, time horizon, income, existing investments, knowledge and experience, attitude to risk, and capacity to absorb loss. The risk profile is recorded in writing and reviewed at least once every twelve months and on the occurrence of any material change in the Client’s circumstances.

10.2 · Suitability assessment

Every recommendation is checked against the Client’s recorded risk profile and stated objectives. The Investment Adviser documents the rationale for each recommendation and maintains the documentation for the period required under the Regulations. Advice is given only where the Investment Adviser is satisfied that the recommended action is suitable for the Client.

10.3 · Client’s obligations

The Client undertakes to (a) provide accurate information at the time of risk profiling; (b) notify the Investment Adviser promptly of any material change in their circumstances; and (c) read the Disclosure Document, the engagement letter, and any product literature provided before acting on advice.

Section 13

Most Important Terms and Conditions.

The following are the Most Important Terms and Conditions (MITC) of the engagement between the Investment Adviser and the Client. These are extracted from the engagement letter for the Client’s ready reference and do not substitute the full engagement letter.

  1. The Investment Adviser is registered with SEBI as a Non-Individual Investment Adviser (INA200013798) and is bound by the SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations, 2013, as amended.
  2. The Investment Adviser provides non-discretionary investment advice. The Investment Adviser does not execute transactions or hold custody of Client funds or securities.
  3. Fees are charged either on an assets-under-advice basis or on a fixed-fee basis, in accordance with the engagement letter, and within the ceilings prescribed by SEBI.

  9. Investment is subject to market risk; the Investment Adviser does not guarantee any return on the recommended investments.
