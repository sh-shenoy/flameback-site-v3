i. THE FRAME
What this page is
and is not

Five composite profiles. Not testimonials.

a.Why composite, why anonymized

We don’t publish client testimonials. Indian securities rules limit what advisers and portfolio managers can say using a client’s words — and honestly, we’d rather keep our clients out of our marketing entirely. Confidentiality is part of the deal.

So this is the next best thing. Each of the five profiles below is a composite, built from real engagements, with details changed and quotes paraphrased to capture the tone of a typical conversation rather than any one person’s exact words. It isn’t here to prove our record — Our Work does that. It’s here to help you see whether you’d belong on this page.

If these situations feel familiar, we’re probably a good fit. If they feel foreign or unappealing, better we both know now.

iii. WHAT THEY SHARE
The mindset the firm’s
clients all carry

Six things our clients have in common. None of them is net worth.

a.Mindset over minimums

We don’t advertise a minimum. Whether a relationship starts at a particular figure is a question for a specific conversation. What actually qualifies someone is a shared idea of what the work is for. These six traits describe it.

i.

Patient money.

Their horizon is decades, not quarters. They don’t need a liquidity event, and they’re not in a hurry.

ii.

Comfort with drawdowns.

They know strategies fall sometimes, and that a fall by design isn’t the same as a strategy failing. They can stay seated.

iii.

A partner, not a product.

They’re not shopping for a return number. They want someone they can trust with complicated decisions over a long time.

iv.

A global outlook.

They think across countries, assets, and generations. India is a big part of the picture, rarely the whole of it.

v.

Self-awareness.

They know the biggest threat to a portfolio is usually its owner — and they want a structure that protects them from themselves.

vi.

An interest in the writing.

They read the letter, the philosophy, the post-mortem. If they don’t, we’re probably not the right fit.

We don’t look for clients of a certain size. We look for clients of a certain temperament. — A line from the firm’s onboarding script, used roughly once a week.
iv. THE FIRST CALL
What an initial conversation
actually looks like

Thirty minutes. No slides.

a.The shape of it

A first conversation is just a conversation. Not a sales call, not a portfolio audit. It’s the two of us working out, honestly, whether this makes sense for both sides. Roughly half of these conversations don’t lead anywhere — and when the fit isn’t there, that’s the point. Better to learn it at minute thirty than month six.

A typical thirty-minute introductory call

The shape of an initial conversation.

0–5 minWhy you’re here. What prompted the call, what you’ve tried before, what you’re hoping a firm like this can solve. The most important five minutes. Most of what follows depends on it.
5–12 minYour situation. Profession, family, business interests, what you already have in place. We ask more than we answer here. No documents needed. No portfolio review. Just listening.
12–20 minHow we work. A plain account of what PWM, Advisory, and QX actually mean for someone like you. What we do, and what we don’t. No deck. The conversation leaves the script fast.
20–25 minThe hard questions. Fees, alignment, conflicts, drawdowns, exit terms. We’d rather field them here than have you find them in writing six months later. Ask the worst question you have. We’ve heard it.
25–30 minAn honest read on fit. Either side can say it’s not there. If we don’t see a fit, we say so and try to point you to someone better suited. No follow-up sequence. No salesperson will call.
